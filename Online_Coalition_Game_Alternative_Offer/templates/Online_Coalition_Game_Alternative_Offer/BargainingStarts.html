You and the other participants will now start bargaining.

To recap, bargaining has V phases:

  • Phase I: Making an offer: Everyone makes an offer indicating which coalition they want to form and indicating how they want to distribute the ${{ total_payoff }} million among the coalition members.
  • Phase II: Choosing offers: Everyone selects the coalition offer they want to execute.
  • Phase III: Seeing chosen offers: Everyone sees which offers are chosen. A tentative coalition is formed when two negotiators have selected the same offer.
  • Phase IV: Making an alternative offer: The negotiator not included in the tentative offer makes an alternative offer to one of the negotiators in the tentative coalition.
  • Phase V: Ratyfing the tentative coalition or accepting the alternative offer. Participants choose to ratify the tentative coalition or accept the alternative offer.
