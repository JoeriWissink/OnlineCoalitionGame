Which coalitions can be formed is determined by the amount of seats received by each party. The amount of seats each party has is determined by a random draw.

Which coalitions can be formed is determined by the number of seats earned by each party. The number of seats a party has is determined by how many citizens of the municipality vote for the party.

Elections are upcoming and all three of you have also been hired to campaign in order to attract more voters. By exerting more effort during the campaign, you increase the chance of getting more seats than the other parties.

Exerting effort during the campaign will be simulated by your performance in a slider task. In this task, the goal is to position as many sliders as you can in the middle of the scale within a given time limit. As the scale goes from 0 to 100, the middle point is exactly 50. If you complete more scales you can earn more seats for your party.

Of course, the other participants will also complete the slider task to earn seats. This means that you will be competing for the amount of seats. The participant that completes most sliders will earn the most seats for their party. The second best participant will earn fewer seats for their party. The participant that completes the least amount of sliders will earn the fewest seats.

The total budget that the governmental coalition has at its disposal will be a fixed amount.
